Overview of FABE Syllabus

The curriculum structure at the Faculty of Architecture & Built Environment (FABE) is designed to integrate robust professional training with general studies. To qualify for graduation, students must satisfy credit allocations across four main components: Core Courses, Elective Courses, University Courses, and Mata Pelajaran Umum (MPU) General Studies.

Core (75% - 80%)

Professional Core Courses

Mandatory technical and professional subjects core to your program. In Architecture, this includes Design Studios, History, and Materials. In Quantity Surveying, it includes Measurement, Cost Planning, and Contract Administration.

📁 Diploma: 77 Credits | Degree: 99 Credits
Electives (5% - 8%)

Program & Open Electives

Specialized optional subjects that allow you to customize your learning pathway or acquire secondary competencies (such as advanced CAD software, construction law, or project management electives).

⚙️ Minimum Requirement: 6 Credits
University (3% - 5%)

Institutional Requirements

Common language and skill courses required by KLUST for all students to foster critical thinking, language proficiency, and communication skills (e.g. English Proficiency and Technical Communication).

🎓 Diploma: 6 Credits | Degree: 3 Credits
MPU General Studies (Malaysian MOHE Requirements)

The Mata Pelajaran Umum (MPU) courses are national general education requirements mandated by the Malaysian Ministry of Higher Education and MQA. They are structured into four distinct groups:

Group U1: Appreciation of Philosophy & Governance (3 - 6 Credits)

Mandatory appreciation subjects covering governance, national identity, and ethics. (e.g., Penghayatan Etika dan Peradaban, TITAS, and Pengajian Malaysia).

Group U2: Core Soft Skills (2 - 3 Credits)

Focuses on personal effectiveness, professional communications, and entrepreneurship. (e.g., Public Speaking, Writing Skills, and Entrepreneurship).

Group U3: Humanities & Cultural Appreciation (2 - 3 Credits)

Encourages social responsibility, general economics, and cultural awareness. (e.g., Malaysian Economy and Integrity & Anti-Corruption).

Group U4: Practical Co-Curriculum (2 Credits)

Experiential community service, volunteerism, sports, or creative arts. (e.g., Co-Curriculum modules).
